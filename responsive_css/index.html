Eating Healthy - Tips from a Type 1 Diabetic

Healthy or Unhealthy? The Choice is Yours!

DISCLAIMER: I am in no way a medical professional or advisor and I am not responsible for your health. This webpage is personal opinion mixed with information obtained from battling over a decade with Type 1 diabetes. Do not self-treat your medical problems using any information provided on this website. This website also contains external links to other websites that are not owne or managed by me.

A Healthy Diet is different for each person due to different body types, and depends on many other health variables such as food allergies, dietary restrictions or intolerances. However, generally speaking, a healthy diet is described using low carb food combinations with lots of greens, vegetables, dairy and natural fruits. Some sources of carbohydrates, such as most vegetables, fruits, whole grains and beans, are healthier than others. Other sources of carbohydrates, such as corns, weats, and breads may not be as healthy (at least for diabetics). For example, sweet potatoes and small red potatoes are healthier than white potatoes. Green beans are lower carb than corn or sweet peas. Of course choosing an apple would be much healthier than 1/2 of a Twix candy bar any day. For diabetic meal planning, 1 carb serving is about 15 grams of carbs. For my specific body weight and height, I was recommended to eat around 45-60 grams of carbs per meal. Breakfast is the most important meal of the day with the higher carb count of 60. Lunch and dinner, I try to eat lighter to not feel sluggish and tired while food is digesting and insulin is absorbing.

IT'S ALL ABOUT MAKING GOOD CHOICES!!

  • CDC- Eating Healthy
  • Harvard School of Public Health - What Should I Eat?

To stay healthy we should all try to choose healthy foods in our diet. A balanced meal should contain many sources of vitamins, nutrients and protein. A Twix candy bar, for example, has 34 grams of carbohydrates... that is almost a whole meal for me personally! No way! A full dinner plate, with salad, steak, baked potato and condiments would be about the same amount of carbohydrates. Therefore, it all depends on healthy food choices!

FRESH FRUITS AND VEGETABLES

  • Eating Well - Here's why you should eat fruit even though it contains sugar
  • American Heart Association - Fresh, Frozen or Canned Fruit and Vegetables: All can be healthy choices

Fresh fruits and vegetables are highly recommended with every meal. Yes, fruits have sugar, but it is natural sugars that your body craves. Eating is all about portion control. A small portion of each food group on the Food Pyramid is really all you need to stay fit.

SWEETS AND DESSERTS

Everyone needs a cheat day sometimes! Sweets and Desserts should be taken in moderation, but not entirely avoided, unless your doctor tells you otherwise. I personally hate when someone says that I can't have something sweet because I'm diabetic. or when someone says "You shouldn't have that, should you?" it takes everything for me not to say "You shouldn't have that either buddy!" Some people may even say that sweets are good for the soul!
