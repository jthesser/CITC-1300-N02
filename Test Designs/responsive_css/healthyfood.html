IT'S ALL ABOUT MAKING GOOD CHOICES!

  • CDC- Carb Counting
  • Harvard School of Public Health - What Should I Eat?
Do you want Healthy Recipes?

To be or stay healthy we all should try hard to choose healthy foods for our daily meals and especially snacks. A Twix candy bar, for example, has 34 grams of carbohydrates... that is almost a whole meal! A full dinner plate, with salad, steak, baked potato and condiments would be about the same amount of carbohydrates and much more filling. It all depends on YOU making healthy food choices while also being aware of portion control! I recommend to start with smaller portions on your plate and then going back for second helpings only if you really feel the need. You will be surprised on how well you actually can feel after eating a lower carb and smaller portion meal compared to being so stuffed you cannot move and being sleepy or sluggish.

BALANCE PROTEINS and VEGETABLES

  • American Heart Association - Picking Healthy Proteins
  • WebMD - Health benefits of eating fish
Do you want healthy Fish Recipes

A full dinner plate balanced with protein, fruits, vegetables, and grains is much more satisfying and healthy than processed foods. Eating fish, just one or two times a week, has proven enough to gain the nutritional benefits found in the fish. Fish is a low calorie, high protein food. It is a great source of omega-3 fatty acids, which also help promote brain growth and development. Omega-3s have been known to decrease the risk of heart disease and decrease inflammation. Fish are high in Vitamin D and other important nutrients and eating fish has been linked to reduce the risk of Type 1 diabetes! NOTE: There are some fish that are not meant to be eaten due to high levels of mercury. See EPA.GOV Article for more information.

SALADS and NATURAL FRUITS

  • Taste of Home - Healthy Salad Recipes
  • Eating Well - Why you should eat fruit.
Do you want awesome Salad Recipes?

Salads with lots of color and vegetables are a healthy choice including many vitamins and nutrients that your body naturally craves. Add proteins such as chicken or ham as well as a delicious dressing and maybe even some fruit, for a delicious and healthy salad! Fresh fruits and vegetables are highly recommended. The majority of your plate should consist of fresh fruit, colorfull vegetables and mixed proteins. If you want to add breads or crackers that is ok. Make the salad a meal and don't feel bad about it!
