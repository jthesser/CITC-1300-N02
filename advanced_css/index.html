Eating Healthy - Tips from a Type 1 Diabetic

What is considered a "healthy diet"?

DISCLAIMER: I am in no way a medical professional or advisor and I am not responsible for your health.
This webpage is personal opinion mixed with information obtained from battling over a decade with Type 1 diabetes.
Do not self-treat your medical problems using any information provided on this website.

A Healthy Diet is different for each person due to different body types, and depends on many other health variables such as food allergies, dietary restrictions or intolerances. However, generally speaking, a healthy diet is described as a one plate meal containing low carb foods such as protein, lots of greens, vegetables, and natural fruits. Some sources of carbohydrates, such as vegetables, fruits, whole grains and beans, are healthier than others. For example, sweet potatoes and small red potatoes are healthier than white potatoes. Green beans are lower carb than corn or sweet peas. Of course choosing an apple would be much healthier than 1/2 of a Twix candy bar. For diabetic meal planning, 1 carb serving is about 15 grams of carbs. For my specific body weight and height, I was recommended to eat around 45-60 grams of carbs per meal. Breakfast is the most important meal of the day with the higher carb count. Lunch and dinner, I try to eat lighter to not feel sluggish and tired while food is digesting.

IT'S ALL ABOUT MAKING GOOD CHOICES!

  • CDC- Eating Healthy
  • Harvard School of Public Health - What Should I Eat?

To stay healthy we should all try to choose healthy foods in our diet. A balanced meal should contain many sources of vitamins, nutrients and protein. A Twix candy bar, for example, has 34 grams of carbohydrates... that is almost a whole meal for me personally! No way! A full dinner plate, with salad, steak, baked potato and condiments would be about the same amount of carbohydrates. Therefore, it all depends on healthy food choices!

FRESH FRUITS AND VEGETABLES

  • Eating Well - Here's why you should eat fruit even though it contains sugar
  • American Heart Association - Fresh, Frozen or Canned Fruit and Vegetables: All can be healthy choices

Fresh fruits and vegetables are highly recommended with every meal. Yes, fruits have sugar, but it is natural sugars that your body craves. Eating is all about portion control. A small portion of each food group on the Food Pyramid is really all you need to stay fit.
