Eating Healthy - Tips from a Type 1 Diabetic

What is considered a "healthy diet"?

A Healthy Diet is different for each person due to different body types, and depends on many other health variables such as food allergies, dietary restrictions or intolerances.

However, generally speaking, a healthy diet is described as a one plate meal containing low carb foods such as protein, lots of greens, vegetables, and natural fruits. Some sources of carbohydrates, such as vegetables, fruits, whole grains and beans, are healthier than others. For example, sweet potatoes and small red potatoes are healthier than white potatoes. Green beans are lower carb than corn or sweet peas. Of course choosing an apple would be much healthier than 1/2 of a Twix candy bar. For diabetic meal planning, 1 carb serving is about 15 grams of carbs. For my specific body weight and height, I was recommended to eat around 45-60 grams of carbs per meal. Breakfast is the most important meal of the day with the higher carb count. Lunch and dinner, I try to eat lighter to not feel sluggish and tired while food is digesting.

IT'S ALL ABOUT MAKING GOOD CHOICES!

  • CDC- Eating Healthy
  • Harvard School of Public Health - What Should I Eat?

As a healthy human we should all try to choose healthy foods for the majority of our diets. A balanced meal should contain approx 45 grams of carbohydrates per meal. A Twix candy bar, for example, has 34 grams of carbohydrates... that is almost a whole meal! A full dinner plate, with salad, steak, baked potato and condiments would be about the same amount of carbohydrates. It all depends on healthy food choices!
