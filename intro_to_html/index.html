What is Type 1 Diabetes?

An autoimmune disease, currently treatable, but still without a cure. (the body attacks itself by mistake). This reaction destroys the cells in the pancreas that make insulin.
Normally diagnosed as a teenager or early in life. The pancreas does not make enough insulin or very little insulin. Diet and exercise are important but only insulin can help regulate blood sugars.

Diabetes Statistics 2024 Update

Q: All diabetes is the same, right?

A: Not true

  • Type 1 Diabetes
  • 0.55% or Approximately 1.3 million Americans have type 1 diabetes.
  • 10% of diabetics are Type 1 diabetic.
  • Also known as Juvenile Diabetes.
  • Normally diagnosed as a teenager or early in life but can occur at any time.
  • Autoimmune - The pancreas does not make enough insulin or very little insulin.
  • Diet and exercise are important to keep blood sugar regulated and in control.
  • Only insulin can help fully regulate blood sugars.
  • Type 2 Diabetes
  • 8.5% or 21 million American adults have Type 2 diabetes in the US.
  • from insufficient production of insulin which causes high blood sugar.
  • Can occur any time in life.
  • Usually related to poor diet and exercise.
  • Can be controlled with better diet and exercise.
  • Can be controlled with pills / medicine as well as diet/exercise.
  • Gestational Diabetes
  • 2% to 10% of pregnant women develop gestational diabetes in the US.
  • This usually goes away after the baby is born.
  • Pre-Diabetes
  • 38% or 96 million American people have prediabetes.
  • 4 out of 5 don't know they are prediabetic.
  • A simple bloodtest can tell if you are prediabetic

Q: I heard cinnamon and aloe vera juice will cure diabetes?

A: Not true

There is a lot of truth that the foods you eat, and the combination you eat them in, can have a postive impact on regulating your blood sugar. However, there is not any magic "cure" for Type 1 diabetes.
